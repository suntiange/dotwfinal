Overview

The New Territory mission will help us understand worlds at the edge of our solar system by making the first human spaceflight to the Pluto system.

The Journey

New Territory will be launched on Jan. 19, 2026; it will swing past Jupiter for a gravity boost and scientific studies in February 2027, and will conduct a six-month-long reconnaissance flyby study of Pluto and its moons that started in early 2035. Pluto closest approach will occur on July 14, 2035. Sending a manned spacecraft on this long journey is a huge challenge on the life support and the hybernation system.

New Science

Pluto and its largest moon, Charon, belong to a third category known as "ice dwarfs." They have solid surfaces but, unlike the terrestrial planets, a significant portion of their mass is icy material. A human landing on these planets promises to examine the sustainability of artificial life-support systems. New Territory is exploring – for the first time – how deep in the universe humans can reach.
